Waterproofing Drone and FPV Electronics

MG TECH · 2026-06-04

Flight controllers, ESCs, receivers and FPV boards are exposed to rain, humidity, condensation and constant vibration. A thin protective coating lets you fly in more conditions and extends the life of sensitive electronics.

Why drones need coating

A few drops of water on a powered flight controller can cause a short and a crash. Coating the boards creates a moisture barrier so light rain, fog and condensation are far less likely to damage the electronics.

What to coat — and what to mask

Coat flight controllers, ESCs, receivers and VTX boards. Mask connectors, antenna pads, USB ports, motor solder pads that may need rework, and any sensors that must stay exposed (e.g. barometer ports may need special care).

Keep it light

Drones are weight‑sensitive. A thin, even layer protects without meaningfully affecting weight or balance; reserve thicker, reinforced layers for edges and high‑risk repair points only.

Vibration and durability

Vibration loosens solder joints over time. A coating that reinforces joints and resists abrasion helps the board survive repeated flights and handling.

Liquid PCB Plastic Coating is applied at room temperature by dipping, spraying or brush — practical for protecting FPV and drone electronics in the workshop or the field.
